## New Zealand — System Overview

### How to Register in NZ

New Zealand does **not** have a standalone online organ donor register. The two actions that matter:

1. **Tick DONOR on your driver's licence** — question 4C on the NZTA application or renewal form. "DONOR" is printed on the card and stored in the NZTA database.
   - NZTA update line: **0800 822 422**
   - Online: [nzta.govt.nz/driver-licences/getting-a-licence/organ-and-tissue-donation](https://www.nzta.govt.nz/driver-licences/getting-a-licence/organ-and-tissue-donation)
2. **Tell your family** — this is the more important step (see Family Consent data below).

**Critical caveat**: Ticking the licence box is legally an "expression of interest only" — not a binding register. The NZTA database is not routinely accessed by ICU clinicians. Family consent is always required and families can override even a registered decision. The Human Tissue Act contains a clause permitting a formal opt-in register to be established by regulation; as of June 2026 this has not happened.

**Advocacy**: GiveLife NZ continues to push for a formal online register. A Law Society submission on the Organ Donors and Related Matters Bill is on record.

ODNZ contact: **0800 4 DONOR** (0800 436 667)

### Opt-in vs Opt-out

New Zealand operates **opt-in**. A person must actively indicate their wish. Compare:
- England: opt-out since 20 May 2020 (Max and Keira's Law)
- Wales: opt-out since 2015
- Scotland: opt-out since 2021
- Ireland: opt-out since 17 June 2025
- Australia, USA, Canada: opt-in

### Key NZ Statistics (2024)

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| Deceased organ donors | 70 |
| Organ transplant recipients | 213 |
| Tissue-only donors | 72 |
| Active transplant waiting list | 400–500+ |
| Living kidney donors (approx.) | ~83 |
| Living liver donors | ~2 |
| Donors per million population | ~10.58 |
| International comparison (Spain) | 49.38 dpmp |
| International comparison (Australia) | 19.4 dpmp |

**Trajectory**: 36 deceased donors (2013) → 74 (2019) → 70 (2024). Significant growth since 2013 reforms.

**Ethnicity gap**: Positive donation intent — NZ European 66.2%, Māori 36.2%, Pasifika 27.6%, Asian 34.4%. Non-Māori/non-Pasifika dialysis patients received kidneys at 3.5–4× the rate of Māori/Pasifika patients (2019 data). Targeted cultural messaging needed.

**$12 million** allocated by government to ODNZ over four years (2023).

## International Site Analysis

### NHS Organ Donation — England (organdonation.nhs.uk)

**System**: Opt-out (deemed consent) since May 2020.

**Hero headline**: *"2 minutes now could save up to 9 lives"* — best-in-class formula: time-to-complete + maximum impact.

**Family consent rate**: 59% (UK). Even under opt-out, 520 cases in 2024/25 saw families not support donation despite presumed consent; 173 families overruled a registered donation decision. This makes the family conversation equally important under both systems.

**Unique elements**:
- Opt-out registration option on the same page (acknowledges choice)
- Physical Organ Donor Card sent to first-time registrants
- Accessible Easy Read guide
- *"Whatever you decide, please talk to your loved ones. They can overturn your decision if they aren't sure what you want."* — most direct family messaging of any site reviewed

**What you can choose to donate**: Heart, Lungs, Kidneys, Liver, Corneas, Pancreas, Small Bowel, Tissue. You can opt in to all or some.

### DonateLife Australia (donatelife.gov.au)

**System**: Opt-in. Registration via Medicare/Services Australia — one minute, phone + Medicare card required.

**Family consent data** (most powerful statistic for the family conversation section):

| Situation | Family consent rate |
|---|---|
| Family member registered on AODR | **82%** |
| Family knew member wanted to donate (not registered) | **63%** |
| Not registered, wishes unknown | **38%** |

**Implication**: Registration doubles consent rate vs unknown (82% vs 38%). But telling your family alone — even without registration — raises consent from 38% to 63%. **Telling your family is the single most impactful action**.

**Key friction reduction**: "All you need is your phone, Medicare card and one minute."

**2024 statistics**: 527 deceased donors; 1,328 transplants; 19.4 dpmp. Target: 25 dpmp.

**80/36 gap**: 80% of Australians support donation but only 36% are registered. NZ has a similar gap.

### Donate Life America (donatelife.net)

**System**: Opt-in, state registries + National Donate Life Registry (RegisterMe.org).

**Key statistics used in messaging**:
- 110,000+ people waiting for a transplant
- A new name added every 8 minutes
- 13 people die each day waiting
- 1 donor saves/heals more than 75 lives (organs + tissues)

**Distinctive**: Explicitly includes "eyes" in all CTAs — "Register to become an organ, **eye** and tissue donor." Most other sites say "organ and tissue" only.

**Low-friction registration**: iPhone Health App integration; state registry + national overlay.

### Ireland (ika.ie)

**System**: Moved to opt-out June 2025 (Human Tissue Act 2024).

**Most powerful reframe**: *"You are three times more likely to need an organ transplant than to be in a position to donate."* Shifts framing from altruism to personal relevance.

**Best structural family conversation tool**: Physical donor card has a **next-of-kin signature field** — conversation is built into the act of registration. The digital app sends an automated notification to nominated loved ones.

### Canada — Be A Donor Ontario (beadonor.ca / donateyourorgans.ca)

**System**: Opt-in, provincially managed. Ontario: prompted choice at ServiceOntario.

**Best tagline**: *"Leave well so others can live well"* — legacy and purpose framing.

**Best conversation starters** (most actionable of any site):
- "I registered to become an organ donor today"
- "Send a note to the family group chat: 'I registered as an organ donor today!'"
- "Plan a day to meet at home or for coffee and speak from the heart"

**Key statistic**: 6× more likely to need a transplant than to be in a position to donate.

**90/32 gap**: 90% of Canadians support donation; only 32% are registered.

## Cross-Site Best Practices

### Hero Headline Formulas

Best formula: **time to register** + **maximum impact**

| Site | Hero |
|---|---|
| NHS UK | "2 minutes now could save up to 9 lives" |
| DonateLife Australia | "One minute. Phone + Medicare card." |
| Ireland | "One donor can save up to 7 lives" |
| Canada | "Leave well so others can live well" |
| NZ (recommended) | "One donor. Up to 10 lives changed." |

### CTA Patterns

- Always state time to complete ("1 minute" / "2 minutes")
- Include eligibility broadener: "No matter your age, health or medical history"
- Two CTAs: primary action + family conversation
- Phone number prominently displayed

### Family Conversation Section

Every reviewed site treats this as a **non-negotiable dedicated section**, not a FAQ item. NZ's current site buries it in myths (Myth 3). Recommendations:

1. Use the Australia 82/63/38% data — strongest evidence-based argument
2. Include Canada-style conversation starters
3. Acknowledge that families can override registered decisions (NHS is most honest about this) — framed as *why* telling family is even more important than ticking the licence

### Organ + Tissue Listing

Best practice: separate organs from tissues; name each specifically; highlight corneas as available outside ICU (community deaths); include living donation pathway. Tissue donation can help up to 75 people; deceased organ donation up to 8–10.

**NZ-specific additions**:
- Skin (NZ Skin Bank, Auckland) — gold standard for burns treatment
- Living donation: kidney and liver (NZ has ~83 living kidney donors/year, ~2 liver)

### Myths — Composite Priority List

These are the highest-priority myths to address, based on frequency across all sites:

1. "Doctors won't try as hard to save me" → Life-saving and donation teams are separate
2. "I'm too old / not healthy enough" → No age limit; very few conditions prevent all donation
3. "It will ruin my funeral / affect open casket" → No; surgical team treats donors with full respect; tangihanga/marae not affected
4. "My religion is against it" → Most major religions support donation; Māori tikanga can embrace it as aroha
5. "My family will just override me anyway" → They won't if they know your wishes (82% consent rate data)
6. "I have to donate everything" → You can choose which organs/tissues
7. "Ticking the licence is enough" (NZ-specific) → Important but family conversation is the key step

### NZ-Specific Gaps vs International Peers

| Gap | Recommended Fix |
|---|---|
| No online register | Acknowledge clearly; link NZTA licence; feature phone prominently |
| Family conversation buried in myths | Dedicated section with Australia consent data |
| "DONOR" on licence not routinely accessed by ICU | Be transparent; frame family conversation as primary action |
| Ethnicity gaps | Note need for culturally targeted pages (Te Reo, Pasifika) |
| Eye/tissue underemphasised | Highlight skin bank, cornea — expands who qualifies to donate |

## Design Decisions for the ODNZ "Become a Donor" Page

### URL
`/become-a-donor` — explicit route

### Page Sections (in order)

1. **Hero** — amber gradient, headline stat, dual CTAs
2. **Two steps** — Tell Your Family (Step 1, primary) + Tick Your Licence (Step 2)
3. **What you can donate** — organ grid + tissue grid + living donation note
4. **Talk to your family** — dedicated section with 82/63/38% data + conversation starters
5. **Common concerns** — accordion-style myth busting
6. **NZ statistics** — 2024 impact numbers
7. **Stories teaser** — link to /stories

### Key Messaging Pillars

1. **Impact**: One donor → up to 10 people
2. **Family first**: Telling your family is the single most important step
3. **No barriers**: No age limit; almost no health condition prevents all donation
4. **Respect**: Open casket, tangihanga, religious beliefs all honoured
5. **Urgency**: 400+ people waiting; every decision matters
6. **Growth**: NZ donations have doubled since 2013 — momentum is real

### Statistics to Feature

- 70 deceased donors in 2024
- 213 transplant recipients in 2024
- 400+ people on the waiting list today
- One donor can help up to 10 people (organs + tissues)
- Tissue donation alone can help up to 75 people
